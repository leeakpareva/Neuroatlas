NAVADA_9
NeuroAtlas

An interactive 3D brain you can explore, label and simulate — and the first organ in what is becoming a personal health simulator. Built around the NAVADA_9 wearable-neurotechnology research concept.

What it is

A real, anatomically segmented human brain you can rotate, take apart and study. Each region is individually labelled with its name and function, and you can simulate how brain activity changes across everyday and clinical states.

How to use it

  • Select — click any region (or pick from the list) to see its name, system and function, with a coloured leader-line callout.
  • Explore — use the explode slider to pull the brain apart, X-ray to reveal deep structures, and toggle axes/grid.
  • Activity — choose a brain state or everyday activity; involved regions flash and a live heatmap shows what's suppressed or overactive.
  • Telemetry — watch a simulated EEG/ErrP trace, neural frequency bands and per-system activity update in real time.
  • NAVADA AI Doc — ask anything; it knows what you've selected and teaches you more.

The NAVADA_9 concept

NAVADA_9 is a wearable earpiece that reads the brain's involuntary error-related potential (ErrP) — generated in the anterior cingulate — from behind the ear, and feeds it back to an AI in real time. The machine acts, the brain reacts, the AI adjusts: a closed loop running at the speed of thought.

Where this is heading

NAVADA NeuroAtlas is growing into a personal health simulator: load your own data from everyday devices and replay it on the 3D model, so you can see how your body behaved over time and talk it through with doctors and other healthcare professionals.

Reading brain signals needs the NAVADA_9 EEG earpiece, which is still in the research phase — so brain data here is simulated for now, while the heart and lungs can already use real wearable data.

Data & technology

  • Anatomy — Z-Anatomy (CC BY-SA 4.0) / BodyParts3D, 32 curated structures
  • 3D engine — Three.js / WebGL
  • NAVADA AI Doc — NVIDIA NIM · Llama 3.3 70B
  • Your data — EEG via NAVADA_9 earpiece (research phase)

An educational and visualisation aid to support conversations with healthcare professionals — not a medical device and not for diagnosis. Simulations are scientifically-informed dramatisations shown until your own data is loaded.
