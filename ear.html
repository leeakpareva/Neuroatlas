OtoAtlas

An interactive 3D ear you can explore, take apart and watch carry sound from the air to the brain — and part of a personal health simulator that can replay your own hearing data on the model.

What it is

A real, anatomically segmented human ear you can rotate, explode and study. The outer ear, the eardrum, the three tiny middle-ear bones and the inner ear's cochlea and balance organ are each individually labelled with their name and function, and you can watch how sound travels through them at different volumes and pitches.

How to use it

  • Select — click any part (or pick from the list) to see its name, system and function, with a coloured leader-line callout.
  • Explore — use the explode slider to pull the ear apart, X-ray to fade the outer ear and reveal the eardrum, ossicles and inner ear inside, and toggle axes/grid.
  • States — choose a sound state (conversation, loud noise, high/low pitch) or a hearing & balance task (music, whisper, head turn, swallow); the conduction chain buzzes and the involved parts light up.
  • Telemetry — watch live loudness, per-system activity and a frequency-response audiogram update in real time, with loudness, pitch and balance.
  • NAVADA AI Doc — ask anything; it knows what you've selected and teaches you more.

Where this is heading

NAVADA NeuroAtlas is growing into a personal health simulator. Hearing data is increasingly captured by everyday devices — headphone audio-exposure tracking, in-app hearing tests and clinic audiograms — so your own readings could be loaded and replayed here, for example to follow how your hearing across frequencies changes over time.

The goal is a clear visual companion you can bring to appointments to talk through your hearing health with audiologists and other healthcare professionals. It sits alongside the brain, heart, lungs and eye in the wider NAVADA Atlas, all running on the same engine so new organs can be added as needed.

Data & technology

  • Anatomy — Z-Anatomy (CC BY-SA 4.0) / BodyParts3D, 11 curated auditory structures
  • 3D engine — Three.js / WebGL
  • NAVADA AI Doc — NVIDIA NIM · Llama 3.3 70B
  • Your data — headphone audio-exposure, app hearing tests, clinic audiograms

An educational and visualisation aid to support conversations with healthcare professionals — not a medical device and not for diagnosis. Loudness and frequency response are scientifically-informed dramatisations shown until your own data is loaded.
