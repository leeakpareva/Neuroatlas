GlucoAtlas

An interactive 3D view of the organs that control your blood sugar — and part of a personal health simulator that can replay your own glucose data on the model.

What it is

The stomach, duodenum, pancreas, liver, gallbladder and spleen — the organs that turn a meal into fuel and keep blood sugar steady — each individually labelled with its name and role. You can watch how a meal, a sugary drink, exercise or unmanaged diabetes change glucose, insulin and glucagon in real time.

How to use it

  • Select — click any organ (or pick from the list) to see its name, system and role in blood sugar, with a coloured leader-line callout.
  • Explore — use the explode slider to separate the organs, X-ray to fade the liver & stomach and reveal the pancreas behind them, and toggle axes/grid.
  • States — choose a blood-sugar state (fasting, after a meal, sugary drink, exercise) or a process (digesting, insulin response, low blood sugar, unmanaged high); the gut churns, the pancreas pulses, and the organs involved light up.
  • Telemetry — watch a live continuous-glucose curve with the in-range band, per-system activity and the insulin / glucagon / GLP-1 hormones.
  • NAVADA AI Doc — ask anything; it knows what you've selected and teaches you more.

Where this is heading

NAVADA NeuroAtlas is growing into a personal health simulator. Blood-sugar data is now captured continuously by wearable glucose monitors (CGMs) like Dexcom and Freestyle Libre — so your own glucose trace could be loaded and replayed here, for example to see how a particular meal or a walk after dinner changed your levels.

The goal is a clear visual companion you can bring to appointments to talk through your metabolic health with doctors, dietitians and diabetes nurses. It sits alongside the brain, heart, lungs, eye and ear in the wider NAVADA Atlas, all running on the same engine so new organs can be added as needed.

Data & technology

  • Anatomy — Z-Anatomy (CC BY-SA 4.0) / BodyParts3D, 6 curated metabolic organs
  • 3D engine — Three.js / WebGL
  • NAVADA AI Doc — NVIDIA NIM · Llama 3.3 70B
  • Your data — continuous glucose monitors (Dexcom, Freestyle Libre), Apple Health

An educational and visualisation aid to support conversations with healthcare professionals — not a medical device and not for diagnosis. Glucose and hormone levels are scientifically-informed dramatisations shown until your own data is loaded.
