SkullAtlas

A real human skull you can rotate, take apart bone by bone and see through — the bony framework that protects the brain and shapes the face.

What it is

An anatomically segmented human skull built from the Z-Anatomy atlas. All 16 major bones — the braincase, the facial skeleton, the jaw and teeth, and the floating hyoid — are individually coloured, labelled and explained. Explode it to separate the bones, or X-ray the vault to see the delicate bones hidden deep inside the face and base.

How to use it

  • Select — click any bone (or pick from the list) to see its name, region and function, with a coloured leader-line callout.
  • Explode — use the slider to separate the skull into its component bones, then assemble it again.
  • X-ray — fade the cranial vault to reveal the sphenoid, ethmoid, vomer and conchae tucked inside.
  • NAVADA AI Doc — ask anything; it knows which bone you've selected and teaches you more.

Data & technology

  • Anatomy — Z-Anatomy (CC BY-SA 4.0) / BodyParts3D, 16 curated skull bones
  • 3D engine — Three.js / WebGL
  • NAVADA AI Doc — NVIDIA NIM · Llama 3.3 70B
