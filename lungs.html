RespiroAtlas

An interactive 3D pair of lungs you can explore, take apart and watch breathe — and part of a personal health simulator that can replay your own respiratory data on the model.

What it is

A real, anatomically segmented human respiratory set you can rotate, explode and study. Every lung lobe, the trachea and the diaphragm are individually labelled with their name and function, and you can simulate how breathing changes across everyday and clinical states — from a quiet resting breath to exercise to an asthma attack.

How to use it

  • Select — click any part (or pick from the list) to see its name, system and function, with a coloured leader-line callout.
  • Explore — use the explode slider to pull the lungs apart, X-ray to fade the lobes and reveal the trachea & diaphragm, and toggle axes/grid.
  • States — choose a breathing state (deep breath, forced exhale, breath-hold) or an activity (exercise, asthma); involved parts flash and a live heatmap shows what is working hardest.
  • Telemetry — watch a live spirogram, per-system activity and lobe-ventilation bars update in real time, with respiratory rate, SpO₂ and tidal volume.
  • NAVADA AI Doc — ask anything; it knows what you've selected and teaches you more.

Where this is heading

NAVADA NeuroAtlas is growing into a personal health simulator. Respiratory data already exists on everyday devices — pulse oximeters for SpO₂, smart spirometers and breathing-rate wearables — so your own readings can be loaded and replayed here, for example to review how your breathing changed over a week.

The goal is a clear visual companion you can bring to appointments to talk through your lung health with doctors and other healthcare professionals. It sits alongside the brain and heart in the wider NAVADA Atlas, all running on the same engine so new organs can be added as needed.

Data & technology

  • Anatomy — Z-Anatomy (CC BY-SA 4.0) / BodyParts3D, 7 curated respiratory structures
  • 3D engine — Three.js / WebGL
  • NAVADA AI Doc — NVIDIA NIM · Llama 3.3 70B
  • Your data — pulse oximeters (SpO₂), smart spirometers, breathing-rate wearables

An educational and visualisation aid to support conversations with healthcare professionals — not a medical device and not for diagnosis. Spirogram and volumes are scientifically-informed dramatisations shown until your own data is loaded.
