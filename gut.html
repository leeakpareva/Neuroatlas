GutAtlas

An interactive 3D large intestine you can explore and watch move food along — and part of a personal health simulator built around your own gut-health data.

What it is

The colon — ascending, transverse, descending and sigmoid — plus the appendix and the duodenum where the small intestine begins, each individually labelled with its name and role. You can watch peristalsis sweep waste along, and see what changes in constipation, diarrhoea or appendicitis. (The coiled small intestine isn't modelled as a separate part in this dataset.)

How to use it

  • Select — click any segment (or pick from the list) to see its name, system and role, with a coloured leader-line callout.
  • Explore — use the explode slider to separate the segments, X-ray to fade the colon and spotlight the small appendix, and toggle axes/grid.
  • States — choose a gut state (resting, after eating, high-fibre, well hydrated) or a process (peristalsis wave, slow transit, fast transit, appendicitis); a wave travels down the colon and the parts involved light up.
  • Telemetry — watch live motility, per-system activity and a colon-transit wave moving through the four segments, with motility and transit speed.
  • NAVADA AI Doc — ask anything; it knows what you've selected and teaches you more.

Where this is heading

NAVADA NeuroAtlas is growing into a personal health simulator. Gut health is increasingly tracked through symptom and bowel-habit apps and at-home microbiome tests (like ZOE) — so your own patterns could be loaded and replayed here, for example to see how fibre, hydration or a flare-up changes transit over a week.

The goal is a clear visual companion you can bring to appointments to talk through digestive health with GPs, dietitians and gastroenterologists. It sits alongside the brain, heart, lungs, eye, ear and metabolic organs in the wider NAVADA Atlas, all running on the same engine so new organs can be added as needed.

Data & technology

  • Anatomy — Z-Anatomy (CC BY-SA 4.0) / BodyParts3D, 6 curated lower-GI structures
  • 3D engine — Three.js / WebGL
  • NAVADA AI Doc — NVIDIA NIM · Llama 3.3 70B
  • Your data — gut-health & bowel-habit apps, at-home microbiome tests

An educational and visualisation aid to support conversations with healthcare professionals — not a medical device and not for diagnosis. Motility and transit are scientifically-informed dramatisations shown until your own data is loaded.
