LarynxAtlas

A real human larynx you can rotate, take apart cartilage by cartilage and see through — the voice box that guards the airway and shapes every sound you make.

What it is

An anatomically segmented human larynx built from the Z-Anatomy atlas. The major cartilages — the thyroid, the ring-shaped cricoid, the paired arytenoids and corniculates, and the protective epiglottis — are individually coloured, labelled and explained. Explode it to separate the cartilages, or X-ray the thyroid to see the moving cartilages tucked inside.

How to use it

  • Select — click any cartilage (or pick from the list) to see its name, region and function, with a coloured leader-line callout.
  • Explode — use the slider to separate the larynx into its component cartilages, then assemble it again.
  • X-ray — fade the thyroid cartilage to reveal the cricoid, arytenoids and corniculates behind it.
  • NAVADA AI Doc — ask anything; it knows which cartilage you've selected and teaches you more.

Data & technology
