MolecularAtlas

Real 3D structures of proteins, pathogens and the drugs that treat them — loaded live from the world's structural databases and explained as you explore.

What it is

A molecular-scale companion to the NAVADA Atlas. Where the organ atlases show anatomy, this shows the machinery inside: viral proteins, enzymes and drug molecules, rendered in true 3D straight from the Protein Data Bank and PubChem. Pick a structure, highlight its working parts, and ask the tutor what each does.

How to use it

  • Structure — pick a protein, pathogen or drug; it loads live in 3D.
  • Sites — click a highlighted site (a binding domain, active site or bound drug) to colour it and read what it does.
  • Display — switch between cartoon, sticks, spheres and a molecular surface; spin to inspect.
  • NAVADA AI Doc — ask anything; it knows which structure and site you are viewing.

How new structures are made

New entries are produced by Claude Science: it predicts or fetches a structure (AlphaFold / ESMFold / the Protein Data Bank), models how a drug binds its target (DiffDock), grounds every description in the literature, and writes a NeuroAtlas-ready catalogue entry. The atlas then renders it here — no manual 3D modelling.

Data & technology

  • Structures — RCSB Protein Data Bank, PubChem
  • 3D engine — 3Dmol.js / WebGL
  • Generation — Claude Science (AlphaFold2, ESMFold, DiffDock, literature-review)
  • NAVADA AI Doc — NVIDIA NIM · Llama 3.3 70B

An educational and visualisation aid — not medical advice and not for diagnosis or treatment decisions.
