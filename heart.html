CardioAtlas

An interactive 3D heart you can explore, take apart and watch beat — and part of a personal health simulator that can replay your own heart data on the model.

What it is

A real, anatomically segmented human heart you can rotate, explode and study. Every chamber, valve cusp and papillary muscle is individually labelled with its name and function, and you can simulate how the heart responds across everyday and clinical states — from rest to exercise to a heart attack.

How to use it

  • Select — click any part (or pick from the list) to see its name, system and function, with a coloured leader-line callout.
  • Explore — use the explode slider to pull the heart apart, X-ray to fade the chamber walls and reveal the valves & papillary muscles inside, and toggle axes/grid.
  • States — choose a cardiac-cycle phase or condition (systole, diastole, tachycardia, AF, infarction); involved parts flash and a live heatmap shows what is working hardest.
  • Telemetry — watch a live Lead-II ECG, per-chamber activity and chamber-pressure bars update in real time, with heart rate, blood pressure and SpO₂.
  • NAVADA AI Doc — ask anything; it knows what you've selected and teaches you more.

Where this is heading

NAVADA NeuroAtlas is growing into a personal health simulator. Heart data already exists on everyday wearables — Apple Watch ECG and heart rate, chest straps and pulse oximeters — so your own readings can be loaded and replayed here, for example to review how your heart behaved over the last week.

The goal is a clear visual companion you can bring to appointments to talk through your heart health with doctors and other healthcare professionals. It sits alongside the brain and lungs in the wider NAVADA Atlas, all running on the same engine so new organs can be added as needed.

Data & technology

  • Anatomy — Z-Anatomy (CC BY-SA 4.0) / BodyParts3D, 17 curated cardiac structures
  • 3D engine — Three.js / WebGL
  • NAVADA AI Doc — NVIDIA NIM · Llama 3.3 70B
  • Your data — Apple Watch ECG / heart rate, chest straps, pulse oximeters

An educational and visualisation aid to support conversations with healthcare professionals — not a medical device and not for diagnosis. ECG and pressures are scientifically-informed dramatisations shown until your own data is loaded.
